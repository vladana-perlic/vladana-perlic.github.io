Residence Le Rabot

I live in a former barracks at about 300 meters above sea level.
The residence description says we have no roommates
But every time I come back from class
Things have been moved around.
The wind is mostly strong at this altitude
So it makes eerie music when it collides with
Old windows, gates
And doors that cannot shut all the way.
It is fairly hard to fall asleep here.
Every night the corridors wake me.
They echo, and from the screams I know:
Someone is dying in the worst agony.
This is a much different place at night.
The electricity often goes out
So we are expected to feel our way to our doors.
Each time I take the same route
Each time the terrain is different.
Sometimes I touch bare stone
Sometimes something that slips away quickly.
The residence staff are here only during the day
And they do not believe in ghost stories.
Except the old porter.
At some hour, the bell at the counter woke him.
A young man in uniform asked for a bandage.
"It has all gone septic, reached the elbow"
He said later.
But he is a drunk
and every so often sees a new apparition.
My friends cannot get over all this
They ask why I have not moved out yet
I really see nothing wrong:
The rent is cheap, the view unsurpassed
And if I already have to share a toilet, shower and kitchen
Better it be with the dead
They are much tidier than the living.
