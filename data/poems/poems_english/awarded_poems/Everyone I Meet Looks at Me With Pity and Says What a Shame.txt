Everyone I Meet Looks at Me With Pity and Says What a Shame

my parents told me
be the best in your class
finish university
and a stable job is waiting for you

when i tried to do something around the house
mom would say leave it, you don't know how, i'll do it
book in your hands, don't end up like me

at twenty-two i tried cooking lunch for the first time
burned my hand on the hotplate
everyone in the student dorm laughed at me
<i>well, you really are useless</i>
straight A's unfortunately could not be eaten

my parents invested
all their hopes in me
they thought something would come of me

they did not know that in real life
no one cares what an appositive is in a sentence
or whether you were a valedictorian

my parents still live
under a regime that no longer exists
they think about pension funds
and have faith in institutions

they say nothing
but on my heart I feel
the whole weight of their thought
<i>at least our son is a doctor</i>
