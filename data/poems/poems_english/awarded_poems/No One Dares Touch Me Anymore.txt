No One Dares Touch Me Anymore

Mother has a phobia of snakes
She doesn't like to see them even on TV
We don't know why
Childhood traumas
I had to tattoo one
across my whole forearm
All the way to my wrist
At Sunday lunch
As I stretched to take the salad
it flicked its tongue from my sleeve
The plate shattered against the heart wall
- a symbol
But this time
Instead of my mother's knotty fingers
My arm was gripped by a powerful house-guardian
The contract was sealed:
We will each guard our piece of pottery
Under the mattresses
That make a sandwich of the Earth
My snake, eternal, terrible, ugly
like my youth
Bites anyone who reaches for my hand
I bark "stay!"
The snake answers "hands are for
grabbing throats"
Mother calls less and less
She cannot lift the receiver
She has cut her hands all over
on her piece of ceramics
"Everything is fine here, Mom
No one dares touch me anymore, Mom"
"No one wants to touch you anymore," says the snake
I shove my hands in my pockets and kick a pebble
The snake says: "Sorry"
I answer: "Once, long ago
You saved my life"
In the old country
At the top of a hill
Beside an abandoned Shokac cemetery
There stands a pomegranate tree
When in the village below the hill
Someone very young dies
The dead rise and around the tree
begin a circle dance
"I hear them sometimes," says the snake
"They call your name"
That is why I stopped buying pomegranates on sale
It kept addressing me, saying:
I see everything
Someone knocks twice on the tower door
Hoofbeats and neighing
A bite on the leg
In a rainy night without stars
A stranger with a scar instead of a mouth
Will recognize you
I see everything
Mother is crying at the other end of the world
She can no longer touch you
I see everything
Pieces of pottery, a contract in blood
You will not let yourself be touched
I see everything
A stranger
A stranger will come
And he will knock a third time
